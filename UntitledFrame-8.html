Description
 The principles of modern biomolecular simulation will be presented in a combined lecture and computer laboratory format. The emphasis will be on establishing an understanding of the strengths and limitations of the computational methods. There are two major projects, which will be performed in the computer laboratory. Project 1 will probe the limits of homology modeling, using a small protein as a case study. Project 2 will examine the ability of computational methods to predict the structures and interaction energies for ligand-protein ligand complexes. Project presentation will be held in the last lab session and each group will have 15 minutes for presentation and 5 minutes for questions.
Software
 The students will gain hands-on experience in using the following computational chemistry programs: AMBER (tLEaP), AutoDock Vina, MOE, and VMD.
Prerequisite
 A familiarity with protein structure and basic physics or chemistry will be useful.
